Unit 1 : Atomic Structure and Properties

Topics

1.1 Moles and Molar Mass

Molar mass: Converts quantities between mass units(grams) and count units (modes)

  • Mass in grams of a substance
  • Exp: 1 mol C = 12.0g C

1.2 Mass Spectra of elements

  • Mass isotope * (percentage); then add all the isptope percentages together
  • Use the periodic table to find elements
  • Make sure the isotope graph (if given) also line sup to what value you got

1.3 Elemental Compositions of Pure Substances

All pure substances have a fixed composition

  • Percent composition: Percent by mass of each element in a compound
  • Empirical formula: Simplest whole number ratio of atoms of each element in a compound
  • Molecular formula: Actual number of atoms of each element in a compound

1.4 Composition of Mixtures

  • Mixture: Physical combination of two or more substances
  • Homogeneous mixture: Uniform composition throughout (solution)
  • Heterogeneous mixture: Non-uniform composition throughout

1.5 Atomic Structure and Electron Configuration

Coulomb's Law: Opposite charges attract, like charges repel

  • Electrons clsoer to the nucleus take more energy to remove while electrons on the outer shells are easier
  • larger q, larger force
  • larger r, smaller force
  • Ionization energy - the amount of energy it takes to remove an electron; increases as you go to the right

1.6 Photoelectron Spectroscopy

  • Measures the energy required to remove electrons from different orbitals in an atom
  • Provides information about the electron configuration and the relative energies of the orbitals
  • Shell/energy level: the coefficient in 1s^2
  • Subshell: the letter in 1s^2
  • Inner - Core electrons
  • Outer - Valence electrons

Photoelectron Spectroscopy

  • Determines relative enerrgies of electrions/ions
  • uses radiation to remove electrons
  • valence easier to remove
  • core harder to move
  • On the left it starts at the ones that take the most energy to remove (1s^2) all the way to the right (Highest EC)
  • Binding energy depends on coloumb's law which states that electrons closer together to the nucleus are harder to remove so the closer ones have greater bond energy
  • If test asks you for the peak of an element 1 proton more, shift the peak to the left since there will be a stronger electron & proton attraction smushign everything together

1.7 Periodic Trends

  • Atomic radius: Size of an atom; decreases across a period (left to right) and increases down
  • Ionization energy: Energy required to remove an electron from an atom; increases across and decreases down
  • Electronegativity: Ability of an atom to attract electrons in a chemical bond; increases across and decreases down
  • Electron affinity: Energy change that occurs when an atom gains an electron; becomes more negative across and less negative down

1.8 Valence Electrons and Ionic compounds

  • Valence electrons: Electrons in the outermost shell of an atom; involved in chemical bonding
  • Ionic compounds: Formed when atoms transfer electrons to achieve a full valence shell; consist of positively charged cations and negatively charged anions
  • Group 1 Alkalai
  • Group 2 Alkaline Earth Metals
  • Group 17 Halogens
  • Group 18 Noble Gases
